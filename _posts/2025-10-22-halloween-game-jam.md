# GameDev.tv Halloween 2025 Game Jam

The GameDev.tv Halloween 2025 game jam ran for 10 days—but I only had about 3 days to put something together.

This was also my **first game jam using Unity**, so there was definitely a learning curve involved.

---

## The Constraints

The rules were simple but challenging:

- only freely available assets could be used
- limited time to build and submit a playable game

With such a tight timeline, I had to scale things back quickly and focus on getting something functional rather than polished.

---

## What I Built

The result was a very simple experience:

- an escape room–style puzzle game
- built around a single puzzle

There were quite a few things missing:

- no start screen
- no end screen
- no music
- minimal polish

It was probably one of the quickest games submitted in the jam.

---

## What Went Right

Despite the limitations, I did accomplish the most important goal:

👉 I finished and submitted a game.

That alone made the experience worth it.

More importantly, the **core idea behind the game still feels strong**, and that’s something I can build on.

---

## What’s Next

Once the jam ends, I plan to go back and expand on the concept:

- add more puzzles
- improve the overall experience
- polish the presentation

This version was just the starting point.

---

## Play the Game

You can check out the submission here:

👉 [Static Souls on itch.io](https://stannellc.itch.io/static-souls)

---

## Final Thoughts

For a first jam—especially with limited time and a new engine—I’m calling this a win.

It may not be polished, but it exists, and that’s the hardest part.

Now it’s time to build on it.
